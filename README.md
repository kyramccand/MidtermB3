<h1>Drive Them Out of Business!</h1>

Race through a busy city in a wild and broken ice cream truck. Your mission?
Crash into ice cream stores and destroy them while avoiding chaos along the way.
Drive fast, take risks and beat the clock.

<h2>Story/Setting</h2>

It is 1999, and you are the sole great-great-great grandchild of Bob Cream, the 18th-century pioneer of ice cream.
Bob's original Ice Cream Truck was a mobile source of joy, an art form in the way it delivered sweet treats to
children all over the neighborhood. The way that Dairy Queen, J.P. Licks, and Scoop N Scootery confine ice cream
to shops disgusts you. Your mission is to free the ice cream from these prisons by smashing into them, while
carefully keeping damage to the surroundings at a minimum.

<h2>Gameplay</h2>

In a top-down chaotic driving simulator, you must get from the start to the ending ice cream store in as
short a time as possible, being rewarded for risks and punished for damages.


<h2>Mechanics</h2>

WASD commands (‘a’ = left, ‘d’ = right, ‘w’ = forwards, ‘s’ = backwards) 
Panels on the ground give you an added boost of speed, and bonus subtracted time.
Hitting pedestrians, mailboxes, other cars, and non-ice cream buildings results in added time penalties.
Picking up little ice cream icons on the road subtracts a bonus small amount of time.

<h2>User Interface</h2>
The camera follows the ice cream truck's position and orientation on the map. The color red is used to
indicate negative consequences (such as hitting a building), while the color green is used to indicate
positive ones (such as destroying the ice cream store. There is a timer in the top right corner of the
screen showing how long the user takes to destroy the ice cream store.

<h2>Game Art/Audio</h2>

The game will have a bright, colorful style representing the 1990s. The city will include roads, sidewalks,
ice cream shop buildings, traffic lights, street signs, and everyday objects like mailboxes, lamp posts, trash
cans, and road cones. There will be cars and pedestrians moving around. The interface will show the score and timer.
The audio will include engine sounds, crashes, breaking glass or car horns, pedestrians reactions. The art
will be created in Procreate, Photoshop and Unity 2D.

<h2>Tech Stack</h2>

Drive Them Out of Business! was built in a 4 week sprint, focusing on
playability, game feel, and a retro 90s aesthetic.
